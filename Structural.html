How To Change Disck Brake Pads on a Car

Changing your own brakes cane be easy, fun and save you big money.

Tools and Parts Needed

  • Replacement Brake Pads
  • Jack and jack stands
  • Lung wrench
  • Wheel Chocks
  • C Clamp
  • Scocket wrench
  • Caliper grease
  • Anti Brake Squeal
  • Brake fluid
  • Optional plastic gloves

Instructions

Removal

  1. ***Put on plastic gloves***
  2. With the car off and in park, use the lug wrench to brake or lossen the lugnuts on the drivers side of the car.
  3. Jack the car up on the divers side just high enough for the tire to spin freely.
  4. Secute the jack stand under the car to saftly hold the weight of the car.
  5. Lower the jake to pull the weight of the car on the jake stand.
  6. Remove jack.
  7. Repeat steps 2 - 6 on the passangerside.
  8. Secure wheel chucks behind the rear tiers to prevent the car from rolling a way. (Safty 1st).
  9. Use the lug wrench to remove the lugnuts from both front tiers.
  10. Remove both tier and place them to the side.
  11. Open and sicure the hood of the care.
  12. Remove brake fluid reservoir cap.
  13. Use the C-Clamp on the brake caliper to push the piston some to lossen it from the brake rotor.
  14. Use the socket wrench to lossen and remove the caliper pins (blots).
  15. Remove the brake caliper from around the brake rotor.
  16. Push the piston the remainder on the way back into the brake caliper with the C-Clamp. (DO NOT OVER DO IT!)

Installtion

  1. Apply anti brake squeal to the back of the brake pads to provrnt friction noise.
  2. Secure the new brake pads to the brake caliper.
  3. Place the brake caliper around the brake rotor.
  4. Apply caliper grease to the caliper pins before inserting them into the brake caliper.
  5. Use the socket wrench to tighten and secure the brake pads and caliper in place.
  6. Lift the tire and put it back on the lugs.
  7. Hand tighten the lugnuts into position.
  8. Use the lug wrench to lightly tighten the lugnuts.
  9. Jack the car up on the driverside to remove the weight from the jack stand.
  10. Remove the jack stand on this side.
  11. Lower the jack placing the tire fully on the ground.
  12. Tighten the lugnuts on this side with the lug wrench.
  13. Repeat steps 24 - 28 on the pasangers side.
  14. Remove wheel chocks.
  15. Replace the brake fluid resevior cap.
  16. Start the car.
  17. Pump brake paddle until it becumes tight.
  18. Turn off the car.
  19. Remove the brake fluid resevior cap.
  20. Add brake fulid to resore the resevior to the proper levale.
  21. Replace the brake fluid resevior cap.
  22. Close the hood of the car.
  23. Start the car and tke for a test drive
